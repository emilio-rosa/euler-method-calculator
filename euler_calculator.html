How It Works

1
Start at a known point

You give the calculator a starting point (x₀, y₀) on the solution curve — this is your initial condition.

2
Measure the slope

The differential equation dy/dx = f(x, y) tells you the slope of the curve at any point. Plug in the current (x, y) to get the slope.

3
Take a small step

Walk a tiny distance h along that slope to reach a new approximate point. The smaller h is, the more accurate the approximation.

4
Repeat

Use the new point as your starting point and repeat the process as many times as you like to trace the curve.

It is often more useful to write this as an iterative formula:
yr+1  ≈  yr  +  h ( dy dx ) r ,  r = 0, 1, 2, …
